Chapter 1: The Hiring

Marcus Ellison had interviewed dozens of candidates
for the open executive assistant position. Some had
been extremely qualified. Some had been extremely
nervous. One had attempted to bring a PowerPoint
presentation explaining why color-coded spreadsheets
were the foundation of modern civilization.

Jennifer was none of those things.

She walked into the office like she already worked
there, set her bag down, and looked around the CEO's
office with casual curiosity.

"So this is where the magic happens," she said.

Marcus blinked. "The... quarterly financial
reporting happens here."

"Right," she said. "Magic."

He checked her resume again. Perfect qualifications.
Excellent references. Former assistants had described
her as "efficient," "organized," and "somehow capable
of running an entire department from a desk the size
of a coffee table."

Marcus gestured to the chair across from his desk.
"Please, have a seat."

Jennifer sat down, crossed one leg over the other,
and leaned forward slightly.

"So," she said. "What exactly do you need from an
assistant?"

"Scheduling. Communication. Document preparation.
Meeting coordination."

Jennifer nodded thoughtfully.

"And occasional crisis management when you forget
what meeting you're supposed to be in," she added.

Marcus frowned. "I don't forget meetings."

Jennifer gently turned the tablet on his desk
toward him.

A calendar reminder blinked on the screen.

Board Strategy Meeting
Started: 10 minutes ago

Marcus stared at it.

Jennifer smiled.

"Relax," she said. "I already emailed them and said
you were reviewing final numbers."

"You just walked in."

Jennifer shrugged. "Multitasking."

Marcus slowly leaned back in his chair.

"How did you even--"

"I like to be proactive."

There was a long pause.

Marcus looked at the resume again. Then at Jennifer.
Then back at the calendar reminder she had somehow
handled before the interview had technically even
begun.

"You know what," he said finally. "You're hired."

Jennifer grinned.

"Great," she said. "Also your tie is crooked."

Before Marcus could react she reached across the
desk, straightened it, and stepped back with a
satisfied nod.

"See?" she said. "We're already improving executive
presentation."

Marcus had the strange and immediate feeling that
his office was no longer entirely under his control.

He would later discover that this feeling was
extremely accurate.

Chapter 2: The Board Meeting

Three weeks later, Marcus Ellison was attempting to
present a major strategic shift to the company's
board of directors.

The boardroom was filled with serious people in
expensive suits. Charts covered the screens.
Financial projections filled the printed reports.

Marcus stood at the head of the table.

"Ladies and gentlemen," he began, "as you can see
from the projections, expanding into the logistics
automation market will require significant upfront
investment, but the long-term--"

The door opened.

Jennifer walked in carrying a tray of coffee.

Every head in the room turned.

Marcus paused mid-sentence.

Jennifer moved around the table calmly, placing
coffee cups in front of each board member with
professional efficiency.

"Good morning," she said pleasantly.

One board member blinked. "Good morning."

Another cleared his throat.

Marcus tried to resume the presentation.

"As I was saying, the projected growth over the
next five years--"

Jennifer leaned over Marcus's chair to place a
coffee on the table beside him.

From this position she casually smoothed down a
stray lock of his hair.

"You had a cowlick," she said.

The room went completely silent.

Marcus slowly turned his head.

"Jennifer."

"Yes, Mr. Ellison?"

"We are presenting to the board."

"I know," she said. "I'm helping you look
professional."

One of the board members coughed loudly into his
hand.

Another was suddenly very interested in the printed
report.

Jennifer straightened and stepped behind Marcus's
chair.

Then, for reasons known only to Jennifer, she
placed both hands on his shoulders and gave them a
brief reassuring squeeze.

"There," she said. "Much better."

Marcus closed his eyes for a moment.

Across the table, one of the board members leaned
toward another and whispered, "Is this... normal?"

The other whispered back, "I have no idea."

Marcus forced himself to continue.

"As you can see on slide twelve--"

Jennifer, apparently satisfied with the coffee
distribution, casually perched on the corner of
Marcus's desk behind him and began quietly
organizing the stack of reports.

Every few moments she would lean forward and point
to a page Marcus had skipped.

"Next chart," she would whisper.

Marcus changed slides.

Another board member raised a hand.

"Mr. Ellison," he said carefully, "before we vote
on the proposal..."

"Yes?"

The man adjusted his glasses.

"Will your assistant be attending... future
meetings as well?"

Marcus stared at him.

Jennifer smiled brightly.

The room grew quiet again.

Finally the chairman of the board cleared his
throat.

"All in favor of approving Mr. Ellison's proposal?"

Hands immediately went up around the table.

The motion passed unanimously.

Marcus slowly turned his head toward Jennifer.

She gave him a cheerful thumbs-up.

"See?" she whispered. "Teamwork."
